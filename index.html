About

I am a Master’s student in Financial Engineering at the University of Southern California with a strong interest in quantitative modeling at the intersection of probability, finance, and real economic systems. My work is driven by a desire to understand how abstract mathematical structures such as stochastic processes and statistical models translate into observable financial behavior in markets and real assets. I am particularly motivated by problems where uncertainty, dynamics, and human behavior interact, and I enjoy building models that are not only technically sound but also interpretable and grounded in economic intuition.

Research Interests

My research interests center on stochastic processes, machine learning, and applied econometrics as tools for analyzing complex financial and economic systems. I am especially interested in Monte Carlo simulation, probabilistic modeling, and data-driven methods for understanding asset dynamics, market regimes, and risk. In parallel, I am drawn to housing and mortgage markets, including mortgage-backed securities and prepayment behavior, where structural modeling and empirical analysis play a critical role. More broadly, I am interested in probability theory as a unifying language for modeling uncertainty across financial markets and real-world economic phenomena.
